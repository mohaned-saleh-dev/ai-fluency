The Thesis

"IQ is what you can do with your mind. EQ is what you can do with people. AiQ is what you can do with AI."

A third capability is becoming essential alongside IQ and EQ. AiQ (AI Quotient) is a structured measure of AI fluency - how effectively a person uses AI tools to improve the quality, speed, and impact of their work. It is not about technical expertise. It is about practical proficiency.

Cognitive
IQ
Intelligence Quotient
How well you reason, solve problems, and process information.
Emotional
EQ
Emotional Quotient
How well you navigate emotions in yourself and others.
AI Fluency
AiQ
AI Quotient
How effectively you use AI to improve the quality, speed, and impact of your work.

Key distinction: AiQ measures augmentation, not automation. High-AiQ individuals use AI to extend their existing expertise, not to substitute for skills they have not developed.

Four Maturity Levels

AiQ is assessed on a four-level scale. Each level represents a qualitative shift in how someone works with AI.

AiQ 1
Aware
0 – 25
Uses AI for occasional tasks. Needs guidance.
AiQ 2
Proficient
26 – 55
AI is part of daily work. Picks the right tool, evaluates output.
AiQ 3
Builder
56 – 80
Designs AI workflows for self and team. Teaches peers.
AiQ 4
Strategist
81 – 100
Drives AI strategy and adoption at organizational level.

Design principle: The levels measure the sophistication of someone's relationship with AI, not their technical depth. A marketing lead who redesigns their team's process around AI (AiQ 3) may score higher than a developer who only uses AI for code autocomplete (AiQ 2).

Six Dimensions of AiQ 1 of 2

AiQ is a composite of six measurable competency dimensions, each assessed at four maturity levels.

D1AI Awareness & Opportunity Recognition
Understanding what AI can and cannot do, knowing the tool landscape, and identifying where AI creates value.
AiQ 1Knows basic tools; uses one for most tasks
AiQ 2Understands strengths/limits; identifies new use cases
AiQ 3Evaluates tools; proposes team improvements; quantifies impact
AiQ 4Drives AI strategy; evaluates investments vs. outcomes
D2Prompt Engineering & AI Communication
Communicating effectively with AI systems - providing context, constraints, and iterating for consistent output.
AiQ 1Basic, unstructured requests; inconsistent results
AiQ 2Provides context and constraints; iterates effectively
AiQ 3Designs reusable templates; manages multi-step interactions
AiQ 4Creates prompt standards and best practices for the org
D3Output Evaluation & Critical Judgment
Critically assessing AI output - catching errors, bias, and quality gaps before they reach stakeholders.
AiQ 1Accepts output with minimal review
AiQ 2Reviews and edits; catches factual errors
AiQ 3Builds systematic quality checks into workflows
AiQ 4Designs org-wide quality standards for AI output

Six Dimensions of AiQ 2 of 2

Dimensions 4 through 6 capture workflow integration, creative output quality, and responsible governance.

D4Workflow Design & Integration
Embedding AI into work processes - knowing which parts of a task benefit from AI and which require human judgment.
AiQ 1Uses AI for one-off tasks, separate from workflow
AiQ 2AI woven into regular work; breaks tasks into AI/human parts
AiQ 3Designs multi-step workflows; uses templates and automations
AiQ 4Redesigns org processes to be AI-native; architects cross-team systems
D5Clarity, craft & output fit (what others see)
Quality, clarity, and appropriateness of AI-supported work that others read or rely on (emails, specs, support, reviews, etc.)—not “brand” as marketing only. GTM roles may add external narrative; the dimension is universal in intent.
AiQ 1Generates drafts; accepts output with minimal personal input
AiQ 2Uses AI as collaborator; output reflects own expertise
AiQ 3Produces work measurably better than human or AI alone
AiQ 4Sets the quality standard for AI-augmented work in their function
D6Risk & responsible use (everyone has a floor)
Data, IP, model/vendor access, and judgment. Every role has a baseline in the same spirit as org-wide security, data, and financial-crime training. Deeper vendor/regulator depth where the role or topic requires it (e.g. risk/legal).
AiQ 1Limited awareness of data risks; may share sensitive info
AiQ 2Follows usage policies; knows what data can/cannot be shared
AiQ 3Designs workflows with compliance built in; trains peers
AiQ 4Shapes organizational AI governance policies and risk frameworks

Scoring & Role-Specific Weighting

AiQ Score = Σ (Di × Wi)
Each dimension scored 0–10, multiplied by a role-specific weight, normalized to 100

Not every dimension matters equally for every role. The weight matrix ensures each function is measured on what matters most to their work.

Assessment & Targets

Four Assessment Methods

Self-Assessment
AiQ Self-Evaluation (AI conversation)
Structured AI conversation: light pass on all six dimensions, with more depth on two (from level × job family weights in product). ~10 min. Reflection and coaching — not a hiring decision in the pilot. One-page read; optional PDF from the tool.
Current Employees
Manager Assessment
Manager Assessment (AI conversation)
Structured AI conversation on observed AI-related work behaviors, aligned to the same rubric as self-evaluation and cross-referenced. Not a rating form or observation checklist.
Current Employees
Practical Exercise
Role-Specific AiQ Challenge
Timed, realistic work problem solved with AI tools. 30–45 minutes.

Integration with People Systems

AiQ integrates into existing organizational infrastructure - not as a standalone initiative, but as a competency layer embedded across the employee lifecycle.

SystemIntegration Point
Hiring / ATSAiQ Challenge as part of the interview process; minimum threshold per role
Performance ManagementAiQ as a formal competency in review cycles; dimension scores inform development goals
Learning & DevelopmentTraining recommendations driven by dimension-level gaps; targeted, not generic
Promotion CriteriaMinimum AiQ threshold as one factor in promotion decisions
Workforce PlanningAiQ distribution by team informs hiring priorities and team composition
