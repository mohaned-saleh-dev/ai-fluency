# Friday Partner Call — Preparation Brief

**Date:** Friday, April 11, 2026
**Context:** COO intro call with US-based founder/company who will help build the AIQ (AI Quotient) initiative. You know almost nothing about them — this call is discovery.

---

## Your Objectives for This Call

1. **Understand** what they bring to the table — product, consulting, content, or a combination
2. **Assess fit** against the AIQ framework you've designed
3. **Define scope boundaries** — what you want them to own vs. what stays internal
4. **Establish credibility** — show you've done the strategic thinking; you're not starting from zero

---

## What You Should Share (Briefly)

Open with a 3-minute framing of the initiative so they understand the context:

- "We're building an enterprise-wide AI fluency program called AIQ — AI Quotient"
- "It covers two populations: new hires (assessment) and existing employees (upskilling)"
- "We have a 4-level maturity model: Aware → Proficient → Builder → Strategist"
- "We're looking for a partner to help us build the assessment and measurement infrastructure — we're taking a hybrid approach: buy the platform, customize the content"
- "This sits alongside a broader AI Transformation Taskforce — AIQ is the people and capability arm"
- "There is no comparable solution globally — we're aware we're building something new"

---

## Discovery Questions — What You Need to Learn

### About Their Company & Product

1. What exactly have you built? Is it a platform, a methodology, a content library, or consulting services?
2. Who are your current clients? What industries and company sizes?
3. How far along is the product — is it production-ready or still being developed?
4. What's your team composition? Technical builders, consultants, content creators?
5. How did the COO come across you / what's the connection?

### About Their Capabilities vs. Your Needs

6. Can you support **multi-dimensional assessment** — not just a quiz, but role-specific, seniority-adjusted assessments that test actual AI tool proficiency?
7. Do you have experience designing assessments per **job family** (engineering vs. product vs. marketing vs. finance)?
8. Can your platform integrate with existing **ATS** (Applicant Tracking Systems)?
9. Do you support both **candidate assessment** (pre-hire) and **employee assessment** (existing workforce)?
10. Can you handle the **measurement/dashboard** layer — tracking adoption, progress, and ROI over time?

### About Customization & Localization

11. How customizable is the content? Can we define our own maturity model, competency framework, and role-specific criteria?
12. Have you worked with companies in the **GCC/MENA region**? Any understanding of local labor law implications?
13. Can the platform support **multi-language** needs?
14. How do you handle **data privacy and security** — especially for candidate data?

### About the Engagement Model

15. What does a typical engagement look like — timeline, phases, deliverables?
16. What's the pricing model — SaaS subscription, per-assessment, project-based, or a combination?
17. What would you need from us to get started? What's the minimum viable scope?
18. Are you open to a **pilot** approach — start with 2-3 job families before scaling?

### About What They Can't Do (Equally Important)

19. What's NOT in your scope? What would we need to build ourselves or source elsewhere?
20. Do you handle the **training/upskilling** side, or only assessment?
21. Can you support **performance integration** — feeding AIQ scores into our existing performance management system?

---

## Scope Framework — What You Want Them to Build vs. What You Own

| Area | Ideal Partner Scope | We Own |
|------|-------------------|--------|
| Assessment platform | Build/provide the testing infrastructure | Define the competency framework and maturity model |
| Content | Provide base content library | Customize for our job families and context |
| Candidate integration | ATS integration, scoring | Interview process design, hiring decisions |
| Employee assessment | Self-assessment + manager assessment tools | Performance integration, review cycle design |
| Dashboards | Adoption tracking, usage analytics | Defining KPIs, targets, and reporting cadence |
| Training content | If available — bonus | Learning path design, internal curriculum |
| Localization | Platform support | GCC-specific compliance and legal review |

---

## Red Flags to Watch For

- They only do generic AI quizzes (not role-specific, competency-based assessment)
- No customization capability — it's take-it-or-leave-it
- No production clients yet — too early stage for your timeline
- They can't support both candidate AND employee assessment
- Pricing is entirely opaque or requires massive upfront commitment
- They want to own the methodology — you need to own your AIQ framework

## Green Flags

- They've worked with enterprise clients at scale (500+ employees)
- Their platform is modular — you can adopt pieces, not the whole thing
- They understand that "AI fluency" is different per job family
- They're open to a pilot-first approach
- They can move fast — your Phase 1 is months 2-3

1. Share the AIQ mind map / framework document (sanitized version)

3. Propose a 2-week scoping exercise to define the engagement
4. Align timeline: their delivery cadence vs. your Phase 0-1 needs
5. Schedule follow-up with relevant internal stakeholders (CTO, CHRO)
